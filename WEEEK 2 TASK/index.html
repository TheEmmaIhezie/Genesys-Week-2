Q: What advice would you give to someone who is just starting to learn how to code?

A: Consistency is key. Practice makes progress, and the more familiar you become with coding, the better you'll get at it. If you experience setbacks, take a break, go for a walk, and come back to it.

Q: What are some steps someone can take to break into the tech industry, especially if they're coming from a non-technical background?

A: Breaking into the tech industry can be challenging, with so many paths to choose from and numerous terms to learn. However, here are a few steps that a close friend taught me and has helped me
  1. Identify your interests: What are you passionate about? What excites you? Identifying your interests will bring you closer to your tech journey.
  2. Learn the basics: You can find many free courses online. Select one that interests you and dedicate time to it.
  3. Get hands-on experience: This can come in the form of boot camps, live instructors
  4. Network: There are many friendly communities online where you can connect with like-minded individuals and learn from and with them.
